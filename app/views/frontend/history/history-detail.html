History of De Adriaan

Unveil the history of Windmill De Adriaan, constructed in the 18th century on the foundations of the Goevrouwetoren. Initially serving as a pivotal defensework, it evolved into a multi-purpose facility under Adriaan de Booys, producing cement, paint, and tanbark. The windmill faced challenges, changing ownership and transforming into a tobacco mill before succumbing to a tragic fire in 1932. Yet, the resilient community rallied, contributing funds for its restoration. In 2002, Windmill De Adriaan, rebuilt on its original foundations, symbolized Haarlem's spirit of unity and determination.

De Adriaan Today

Today, Windmill De Adriaan, stands tall on Scheepmakersdijk. Restored in 2002, exactly 70 years after the devastating fire, the mill embodies Haarlem's resilience. Owned by Stichting Molen De Adriaan, the windmill invites visitors to explore its reconstructed form, bearing witness to a history that includes both adversity and triumphant restoration efforts. A symbol of community strength, Windmill De Adriaan continues to tell the story of Haarlem's enduring spirit.
